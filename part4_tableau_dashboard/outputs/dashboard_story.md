# Dashboard Story (Task 9)
**Project:** Executive Sales Performance Dashboard — Retail Chain  
**Audience:** Senior Leadership Team  
**Period:** January 2024 – December 2025  
**Data:** 4,200 orders | ₹21.7 Crore total sales | ₹3.33 Crore total profit

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The retail chain delivered ₹21.7 Crore in total sales over FY 2024–2025 at a profit margin of 15.4%. Technology is the unambiguous engine of the business — contributing 70.9% of total sales and 84.2% of total profit at an 18.2% margin. Furniture and Office Supplies, while contributing volume, are significant margin drags.

The dashboard reveals a business that is structurally healthy at the top line but faces margin erosion from three specific risks: over-discounting in Furniture and Office Supplies, high return rates in Furniture (7.7%), and slow delivery speeds under Standard Class shipping. The South region leads in absolute sales and presents the clearest opportunity for further investment.

The single most important strategic message from this dashboard: **Technology drives profit, Furniture erodes it. Leadership needs a deliberate strategy to grow what works and fix what doesn't.**

---

## 2. What Is Performing Well

### Technology Category — Engine of the Business
Technology accounts for ₹15.39 Crore in sales (70.9% share) and ₹2.80 Crore in profit. Sub-categories Copiers, Accessories, and Phones each deliver above 18% margins. These are premium products with strong Corporate and Home Office demand. Technology's return rate is the lowest across categories at just 3.0%, indicating high customer satisfaction and product reliability.

### South Region — Volume Leader
The South region generates the highest sales (₹6.47 Crore, 30.0% share), reflecting strong market penetration. South's profit contribution (₹9.99 Crore) tracks proportionally with its sales volume, confirming that South is not achieving sales through excessive discounting. It is a genuine high-opportunity region.

### Customer Segment Diversification
All three segments — Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office — contribute roughly equally (32–34% each) to total sales. This balance reduces dependence on any single customer type and provides resilience against segment-specific economic downturns. Home Office leading slightly (34.3%) reflects a structural post-pandemic shift that continues to benefit the business.

### Same Day and First Class Shipping
Among orders using premium shipping modes, delivery performance is excellent. Same Day averages 0.4 days; First Class averages 1.77 days. Customers choosing these modes receive high-quality service, and their satisfaction ratings trend higher.

---

## 3. What Is Underperforming

### Furniture — High Volume, Low Reward
Furniture is the second-largest category by sales (₹5.16 Crore, 23.8%) but delivers only 6.9% profit margin — less than half the portfolio average of 15.4%. Sub-categories Tables and Bookcases are the weakest performers. Furniture also carries the highest return rate (7.7%) and is likely the category where the deepest discounts are applied to win large orders.

The data pattern suggests that Furniture deals are being chased at the expense of profitability. The combination of high discounts + high returns + low margins creates a triple-compression problem that is suppressing overall portfolio results.

### Standard Class Shipping — Speed and Satisfaction Risk
Standard Class accounts for a significant portion of shipments yet averages 4.71 delivery days — more than twice the Second Class average and more than 11× the Same Day average. In categories where customers expect rapid fulfillment (Technology orders, emergency office supplies), this creates an expectation gap that manifests in return risk and lower customer ratings.

### August Sales Dip — Recurring Trough
August 2024 recorded the lowest monthly sales (₹6.3M) — a 40% drop from the January 2024 peak. This pattern suggests a predictable mid-year slowdown likely linked to pre-festive budget freezes or school-year inventory depletion in education-adjacent customer segments. Without intervention, this trough will recur.

### East and West Regions — Under-Indexed
Both East (₹4.89 Crore) and West (₹4.89 Crore) are at similar levels — notably below North (₹5.46 Crore) and well below South (₹6.47 Crore). The structural gap suggests either weaker market penetration, fewer stores, or suboptimal product mix in these regions.

---

## 4. What Risks Are Visible

### Risk 1 — Discount Spiral in Furniture
Orders with 21–35% discounts generate only ₹2,915 average profit — a 78% reduction compared to undiscounted orders (₹13,203). If Furniture orders increasingly rely on deep discounts to close deals, the category will slide into chronic margin compression.

### Risk 2 — Furniture Return Rate Creating Hidden Costs
At 7.7% return rate (88 returned orders), Furniture is generating significant reverse logistics costs that do not appear directly in the profit column. Each returned Furniture item involves pick-up, damage assessment, restocking or write-off. The true cost of Furniture's already-thin 6.9% margin is likely negative once returns are fully costed.

### Risk 3 — Campaign Attribution Gaps
24 orders (0.6%) have untracked campaign channels. While small today, if this gap grows — particularly across Paid and Email campaigns — marketing ROI reporting will become unreliable, leading to misallocated budgets.

### Risk 4 — Over-Reliance on Technology
With 70.9% of sales and 84.2% of profit in a single category, the business is structurally exposed if Technology demand softens, supply chains tighten, or a competitor offers aggressive pricing on Copiers or Machines.

---

## 5. What Opportunities Are Visible

### Opportunity 1 — Grow Technology in South and North Regions
Given Technology's superior margins, and given that South and North already show strong sales momentum, there is a clear opportunity to deepen Technology penetration in these regions through dedicated corporate account management and high-volume deal structures.

### Opportunity 2 — Rehabilitate Furniture Margin
Furniture doesn't need to be abandoned — it needs repricing and cost restructuring. Setting minimum margin floors (e.g., 12% for all Furniture orders), improving packaging to reduce return rates, and bundling with higher-margin Technology items (e.g., desks + monitors) could significantly improve Furniture contribution.

### Opportunity 3 — Shipping Upgrade as a Differentiation Tool
Offering free First Class shipping for orders above a threshold (e.g., ₹25,000) would serve dual purposes: increasing average order value (customers would add items to qualify) and improving customer satisfaction scores. The shipping upgrade cost on high-value Technology orders is small relative to the margin improvement.

### Opportunity 4 — August Mid-Year Push Campaign
The predictable August trough represents a clear opportunity for a targeted mid-year campaign (e.g., "Back to Office" or "Monsoon Tech Deals") targeting Corporate and Home Office segments for Technology purchases. A 15% uplift in August sales would add approximately ₹0.9M in revenue at portfolio average margins.

### Opportunity 5 — Channel Mix Optimisation
If attribution data is cleaned up, analysis of which campaign channel drives the highest AOV and margin orders (not just volume) could unlock significant marketing efficiency gains. Organic and Referral channels likely deliver higher-quality customers at lower acquisition cost than Paid.

## 6. Recommended Business Actions

| Priority | Action | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (Urgent) | Set discount guardrails: Technology max 10%, Furniture max 15%, Office Supplies max 20% | Protect margin; prevent discount spiral |
| 2 (Urgent) | Investigate Furniture return root causes; improve packaging for Tables/Chairs | Reduce 7.7% return rate; cut hidden logistics costs |
| 3 (Short-term) | Launch August mid-year promotion campaign targeting Corporate + Home Office | Fill seasonal trough; add ~₹0.9M revenue |
| 4 (Short-term) | Implement shipping upgrade policy for Technology orders > ₹50,000 | Improve customer rating; reduce cancellation risk |
| 5 (Medium-term) | Deepen Technology sales coverage in South and North regions | Grow highest-margin category in high-performing regions |
| 6 (Medium-term) | Fix CRM attribution tracking; mandate campaign_channel on all orders | Accurate marketing ROI; better budget allocation |
| 7 (Long-term) | Review Furniture portfolio — consider exiting Bookcases & Tables or repositioning as premium | Improve portfolio margin profile; reduce complexity |

---

## 7. Limitations of the Dashboard

| Limitation | Impact |
|---|---|
| Only 2 years of data (2024–2025) | Cannot reliably distinguish trend from seasonality; 3+ years needed |
| 32 missing customer_rating values (0.8%) | Rating-based analysis is slightly incomplete |
| 24 missing campaign_channel values (0.6%) | Channel ROI calculations are approximations |
| Return data is binary (returned / not returned) | No reason code available; cannot diagnose root cause |
| No cost detail (only derived from sales - profit) | Cannot distinguish COGS vs. logistics vs. marketing cost |
| No store/location granularity within regions | Cannot identify individual high/low-performing locations |
| No competitor or market data | Cannot assess whether South's leadership reflects market share gain or market growth |

---

## 8. Suggested Next Analysis

1. **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Analysis** — Link customer_id across orders to understand which customers are repeat buyers, what they buy, and which segment has the highest retention rate. This would inform segment-specific investment decisions.

2. **Return Root Cause Analysis** — Add return reason codes to the dataset and build a dedicated return analysis dashboard showing reasons by category, shipping mode, and campaign channel.

3. **Shipping Mode vs. Customer Rating Correlation** — Test whether Standard Class shipments are statistically correlated with lower customer ratings using the available data. This would quantify the business case for shipping upgrade investments.

4. **Campaign Channel ROI Dashboard** — Clean up attribution data and build a dedicated channel performance view showing cost-per-order and profit-per-order by campaign channel.

5. **Profitability Forecast** — Build a forward-looking scenario model using the observed seasonality patterns to forecast Q1 and Q3 2026 performance under different discount and product mix assumptions.
